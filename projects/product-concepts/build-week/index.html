PrintKit first. Products on PrintKit. Weird stuff for dessert.
The logic is simple:

if (printkit.works()) then every product idea is a weekend project forever.
if (!printkit.works()) then every product idea is a full sprint forever.

You don't have a product problem. You have 40 products. You have a product velocity problem. PrintKit is the only path that solves the actual bottleneck. Build the factory, then run the factory.

But also — you're 3 builders in a room. The energy matters. If PrintKit turns into infrastructure slog by Wednesday, pivot to shipping one maker tool ON PrintKit, even if it's janky. The goal isn't "PrintKit is perfect." The goal is "one product shipped through PrintKit, proving it works."

// The Uncomfortable Truth
SPS makes $6.3M/yr selling 40+ products with no shared infrastructure. Every new product is a hand-built artisan snowflake. That worked when you were a small team doing small things. It doesn't work when you want to go from $6M to $20M.

The companies that scale product catalogs fast — Shutterfly, Printful, Canva — all built the factory first. PrintKit IS the strategy. Not because it's the most fun thing to build this week, but because it's the thing that makes next week 10x easier, and the week after that 100x easier.

That said: a factory that never ships a product is just a hobby. The week ends with at least one real product live on PrintKit or you've failed. Ship the factory AND the product. That's the game.
